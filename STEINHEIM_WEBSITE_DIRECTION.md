# Steinheim Egypt Website Direction

This document locks the visual and motion direction chosen from the approved image mockup board.

## North star

The website should feel like a premium digital flagship for Steinheim Egypt: architectural, calm, editorial, product-accurate, and serious enough for architects, developers, hotel operators, interior designers, contractors, and high-end homeowners.

It should not feel like a generic ecommerce theme, a SaaS landing page, or a flashy AI demo.

## Visual language

- Palette: near-black `#1A1A1A`, pure white, soft grey, and controlled metal accents.
- Warmth comes through Steinheim photography and real material texture, not through a beige-heavy UI system.
- Typography: large serif editorial headlines, clean sans-serif body, tiny uppercase labels.
- Imagery: real Steinheim lifestyle/product/finish assets first. AI-generated imagery only as temporary concept support.
- Layout: full-bleed hero moments, large product imagery, disciplined spacing, minimal copy, strong grids.
- Tone: luxury through restraint, accuracy, and confidence.

## Site pages

### Home

Purpose: establish premium brand world and route users quickly.

Sections:

1. Cinematic video/photo hero
2. Short brand statement
3. Four collection cards: Joy, Up, Art, Quatro
4. Specification/engineering credibility section
5. Finish palette section
6. Trade/project CTA

Motion:

- Hero video slow zoom
- Navigation fade-in
- Headline line reveal
- Collection card stagger reveal
- Subtle image parallax

### Collections landing

Purpose: help users understand the four design languages before product browsing.

Sections:

1. Editorial hero: "Four collections. One architectural language."
2. Large 2x2 or 4-column collection cards
3. Each card shows role, best use, active finish count, CTA
4. Compare / catalogue / trade CTA

Motion:

- Hero line reveal
- Cards reveal one by one
- Hover: image slow zoom, overlay deepens, title lifts, arrow extends
- Click: soft transition into collection detail

### Collection detail

Purpose: sell and specify products from one collection.

Sections:

1. Collection hero
2. Positioning and best-use statement
3. Product type and finish filters
4. Product grid from verified Egypt catalogue
5. Featured package / project recommendation
6. Add to proposal / compare / catalogue CTA

Motion:

- Product cards fade up in batches
- Filters animate smoothly
- Finish dots appear clearly on hover
- Product image lifts slightly on hover

### Product detail

Purpose: feel like a serious specification page, not a basic ecommerce product page.

Layout:

- 65/35 split on desktop
- Large product image
- Product name, model, price, finish dots, CTAs
- Tabs: Description, Product Detail, Customisation Options, Downloads
- Add to proposal drawer

Motion:

- Finish dot hover/click morphs product image
- Tabs slide/fade
- Add to proposal opens right drawer
- Related products reveal below

### Finishes

Purpose: make finish choice tactile and understandable.

Sections:

1. Hero statement
2. Six finish discs: Chrome, Brushed Nickel, Matte Black, Brushed Gold, Coffee Gold, Metal Gun
3. Finish care and warranty notes
4. Availability reminder: varies by product and collection

Motion:

- Finish discs reveal slowly
- Hover magnifies surface texture subtly
- No spinning or gimmicks

### Interactive catalogue

Purpose: make the catalogue feel like guided exploration, not a PDF dump.

Motion:

- Smooth page/spread transitions
- Product markers glow briefly
- Guided jumps when the assistant or user selects a product

### Build proposal / configurator

Purpose: support trade/B2B workflows.

Features:

- Add products with finish and quantity
- Retail-reference total
- Project notes
- Create RFQ in demo mode
- No real email sending until approved

Motion:

- Drawer slides in from right
- Quantity and total update smoothly

### Trade / Projects

Purpose: establish credibility and make Steinheim easy to specify.

Sections:

1. Trade hero
2. Project/specification workflow
3. Project credibility cards
4. Warranty/support/service proof
5. RFQ CTA

Motion:

- Workflow cards reveal in sequence
- CTA pins visually but should not feel sticky-heavy

### About

Purpose: tell the Steinheim story with authority and restraint.

Sections:

1. Cinematic video/photo block
2. Design, engineering, reliability principles
3. Egypt distribution/support
4. Warranty/service route

### Warranty

Purpose: remove ambiguity.

Structure:

- Sedal cartridge
- Chrome / Brushed Nickel
- Neoperl / body
- PVD finishes
- Egypt-only terms

### Journal

Purpose: credibility and SEO, but secondary.

Content types:

- Care guides
- Finish selection
- Project specification thinking
- Bathroom design advice

### Contact

Purpose: fast contact without clutter.

Routes:

- WhatsApp
- Email
- Trade enquiry
- Showroom/company contact

## Motion rules

Use:

- Slow video zooms
- Text line reveals
- Soft scroll reveals
- Subtle image parallax
- Product finish morphs
- Proposal drawer slide-ins
- Soft page fades/wipes
- Reduced-motion fallback

Avoid:

- Bouncy UI
- Spinning objects
- Flashy scroll-jacking everywhere
- Random animation styles
- Over-fast transitions
- Heavy motion on mobile

## Build sequence

1. Navigation + homepage
2. Collections landing
3. Collection detail pages
4. Product detail template
5. Finishes page
6. Trade/project page
7. Catalogue/proposal polish
8. About, warranty, journal, contact polish

## Decision rule

Every section must pass this test:

Would Karim proudly send this page to an architect, hotel owner, developer, or interior designer?

If not, simplify it, make it more accurate, or cut it.
